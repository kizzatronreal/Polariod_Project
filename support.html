Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Camera Won't Turn On

  • Ensure a fresh film pack is properly installed the battery is in the film pack
  • Check that the film pack is fully inserted and the dark slide has been ejected
  • Try a new film pack if the current one may be expired or depleted

Photos Are Too Dark or Too Light

  • Use the Lighten/Darken control slider to adjust exposure
  • Ensure you're within the recommended shooting distance
  • Clean the Light Management System sensors with a soft cloth
  • Avoid shooting in extremely bright sunlight or very dark conditions

Photos Aren't Ejecting

  • Check that the film counter shows remaining exposures
  • Remove and reinsert the film pack, ensuring it clicks into place
  • Make sure the film pack door is fully closed
  • If photos are jammed, do not force them contact customer support

Blurry or Out of Focus Images

  • Hold the camera steady when pressing the shutter button
  • Ensure minimum shooting distance of 4 feet from subject
  • Clean the lens with a soft, dry cloth
  • Check that your subject is centered in the viewfinder

Film Care & Storage

Proper film storage is essential for best results:

  • Store film packs in a cool, dry place (ideally 40-70°F)
  • Keep film away from direct sunlight and heat sources
  • Use film before the expiration date printed on the package
  • Allow film to reach room temperature before loading if refrigerated
  • Shield developing photos from direct sunlight for 5-10 minutes after ejection

Warranty Information

The Polaroid Sun 600 comes with a one-year limited warranty covering defects in materials and workmanship. This warranty does not cover:

  • Damage from misuse, accidents, or unauthorized repairs
  • Normal wear and tear
  • Film-related issues
  • Cosmetic damage that doesn't affect performance

To make a warranty claim, contact customer support with your proof of purchase and a description of the issue.

Quick Tips for Best Results

  • Always shoot with the sun behind you, not behind your subject
  • Use the built-in flash indoors or in low-light conditions
  • Hold the camera level and steady don't move until the photo ejects
  • Shield developing photos from bright light immediately after ejection
  • Store finished photos in an album away from heat and humidity
  • Clean your camera regularly with a soft, dry cloth
