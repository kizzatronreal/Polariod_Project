Polaroid Corporation

Pioneers of instant photography since 1937

Company History

Founded in 1937 by Edwin H. Land and George W. Wheelwright III, Polaroid Corporation revolutionized photography with the invention of instant film. The company's headquarters was located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, where it became one of the most innovative technology companies of the 20th century.

The introduction of the first instant camera in 1948 marked the beginning of a new era in photography. By the 1980s, Polaroid had become a household name, with the 600 Series cameras representing the pinnacle of instant photography technology.

Our Mission

Polaroid's mission has always been to make photography instant, accessible, and fun for everyone. We believe that capturing and sharing moments shouldn't require waiting days for film development. With Polaroid instant cameras, memories develop right in your hand, creating tangible keepsakes that last a lifetime.

Innovation & Technology

Polaroid has been at the forefront of photographic innovation for decades. Our research and development team pioneered numerous technologies including:

  • Instant film development – Photos that develop in minutes without a darkroom
  • Integral film – Self-contained film packs with built-in chemistry
  • SX-70 technology – Single-lens reflex instant photography
  • 600 Series film – Higher ISO film for better low-light performance
  • Light Management System – Advanced exposure control for clearer photos
